Data Model & System Map

How the PDF data model maps to the CRM, ERP, and QA databases, and how every table participates in the eight executive use cases.

● Open System Health
↻ End-to-end process · Lead → Quote → Order → Invoice → Revenue → Reliability feedback
PDF Source

The application implements the full life cycle from the PDF: lead generation through revenue recognition, plus a quality-and-reliability feedback loop that pushes data back into the CRM as opportunity-level insights.

1 · Lead capture (CRM) → 2 · Account mgmt → 3 · Opportunity → 4 · Quote → 5 · Sales order (ERP) → 6 · Invoice → 7 · Revenue → CRM ⤴ 8 · QA reliability feedback
■ Salesforce CRM (Front office)
11 tables

Owns the customer-facing life cycle: leads, accounts, contacts, opportunities, products & pricebook, quotes & line items. Two sync tables receive feedback from ERP (revenue) and QA (reliability insights).

TableRole
usersSales reps & managers (Salesforce User)
accountsCustomer master, key-account flag
contactsContacts tied to accounts
leadsLead capture & qualification
opportunitiesPipeline incl. close stage
productsProduct catalog with design specs
pricebook_entriesPricelist variants
quotesCustomer quotes
quote_line_itemsQuote details
quote_revenue_syncRevenue pushed back from ERP
reliability_insights_syncQA → CRM insight on product/opp/account
■ ERP System (Back office)
8 tables

Owns transactional & financial state: customer master, item master, sales orders, invoices, payments, GL, and recognized revenue. Integration keys link every row back to the CRM.

TableRole
customersERP customer master · ext_account_id ↔ CRM
itemsERP item master · ext_product_id ↔ CRM
sales_ordersSO with optional ext_quote_id ↔ CRM quote
sales_order_linesLine-level SO data
invoicesCustomer invoices, recognition status
invoice_linesLine-level invoice data
paymentsCash applications
gl_entriesGL journal entries
revenueRecognized revenue (per period, per item)
■ QA / Reliability
9 tables

Owns reliability data: test specs and runs, sample-level results, MTBF/MTTR metrics, failures, customer returns, environmental tests, compliance, and a composite reliability score per product.

TableRole
test_specificationsPer-product test definitions
test_runsExecution of test specs
test_resultsSample-level pass/fail
reliability_metricsMTBF, MTTR, FIT, failure rate by quarter
failuresFailure records w/ root cause
customer_returnsRMAs linked to product + account
environmental_testsThermal, vibration, HAST, etc.
compliance_recordsCertifications · UL · CE · AEC-Q100…
reliability_scoresComposite 0-100 score + grade
🔗 Cross-system integration keys
Joins

Three keys glue everything together — exactly as the PDF integration overview specifies. 

CRM accounts.id     ─────► ERP customers.external_account_id
                    ─────► QA failures.external_account_id
                    ─────► QA customer_returns.external_account_id

CRM products.id     ─────► ERP items.external_product_id
                    ─────► QA test_specifications.external_product_id
                    ─────► QA reliability_metrics.external_product_id
                    ─────► QA customer_returns.external_product_id
                    ─────► QA reliability_scores.external_product_id

CRM quotes.id      ◄─────► ERP sales_orders.external_quote_id
ERP invoices       ─────► CRM quote_revenue_sync (revenue back-sync)
QA reliability_scores ─► CRM reliability_insights_sync (insight back-sync)
